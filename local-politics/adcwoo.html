ADC EYES FIVE GOVERNORS AS SANWO-OLU, WIKE CRITICIZE COALITION LEADERS

EL-ZION NEWS   

The African Democratic Congress (ADC), the new platform adopted by a coalition of opposition figures, is reportedly making efforts to woo no fewer than five sitting governors ahead of the 2027 general elections.

Sources within the party say the ADC hopes to capitalise on internal disputes within the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) to draw some of its governors into the coalition. Already, five PDP leaders from different states have defected to the ADC.

On Wednesday, prominent opposition figures, including former Vice President Atiku Abubakar, 2023 Labour Party presidential candidate Peter Obi, former Senate President David Mark, and others, formally adopted the ADC as their platform to challenge President Bola Tinubu in 2027.

David Mark and former Minister of Interior, Ogbeni Rauf Aregbesola, were named Interim National Chairman and Secretary of the ADC, respectively.

Several influential All Progressives Congress (APC) members such as ex-National Chairman John Oyegun, former Attorney General Abubakar Malami, and former Minister of Transportation Rotimi Amaechi, were also present at the event and subsequently announced their departure from the APC to join the ADC.

Similarly, top PDP members including former National Chairman Uche Secondus, ex-House Speaker Aminu Tambuwal, and former governors Sule Lamido (Jigawa), Liyel Imoke (Cross River), Gabriel Suswam (Benue), Emeka Ihedioha (Imo), Babangida Aliyu (Niger), and Sam Egwu (Ebonyi), have aligned with the coalition.

Ireti Kingibe, a Labour Party senator representing the Federal Capital Territory, also attended the event.

ADC Faces Backlash

Since being declared as the coalition's political platform, the ADC has drawn criticism over its leadership and viability. Acting PDP National Chairman, Umar Damagum, warned of repercussions for party members undermining internal unity, stating the party remained cohesive.

Political commentator and former presidential aide Reno Omokri dismissed the ADC, calling it a group of “rejected politicians and former political office holders.”

APC South-East National Vice Chairman Ijeomah Arodiogbu argued that the absence of any sitting governors in the coalition was a significant weakness that undermined its credibility.

Speaking on the matter, Arodiogbu stated, “The coalition is already starting on a bad footing. The party they have coalesced into is already having issues. Some leaders of the party said they were not consulted and that they don’t want the newcomers. With that, it is obvious that there is already a problem.”

He added, “The lowest common multiple of the people driving the coalition is simply desperation for power. It is not about the Nigerian people. All they have in their agenda is how to pull down Tinubu and the APC. They don’t have anything to offer the Nigerian people.”

Arodiogbu also questioned the relevance of Emeka Ihedioha in South-East politics, describing him as lacking electoral strength.

He maintained that the coalition was attempting to replicate the APC’s successful merger but lacked the necessary momentum, especially the backing of governors. “One fundamental thing that worked for the merger was the joining of six sitting governors. And that is not happening now. Instead, the governors are coming to us. If the governors truly believed in the coalition, they would have joined them by now. But the big political forces are not joining them because they do not believe in them,” he added.

Five PDP Governors Set to Join – ADC Sources

A senior member of the ADC, a former senator from Katsina State, revealed that five PDP governors had committed to joining the coalition after resolving internal issues involving Nyesom Wike.

“Five PDP governors have given us their commitment; they are going to join us, but they want to see the end of Wike’s drama in the PDP. Hopefully, we will meet them by the end of their next convention and review things,” the source said.

He added, “What people don’t know is that some of the governors, including those in the APC, are supporting us. We started this coalition planning 18 months ago, and some APC governors were contacted about a year ago. So, they are with us, but I won’t tell you any names.”

Another insider, an aide to a former minister and ex-governor, said leaders of the coalition were already in discussions with seven governors about joining the ADC, but declined to specify which parties they belonged to.

“Why are you interested in knowing the governors we’re talking to? You want us to put everything we’re doing on the pages of a newspaper? No! But I can tell you that we are talking to seven governors, and they are from the southern and northern parts of the country,” the source stated.
