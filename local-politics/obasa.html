Lagos Politics Feb 27, 2025

Speaker Obasa, who has been impeached, entered the premises of the Lagos Assembly with a significant security detail.

El-zion Global Network News reported that the impeached Speaker of the Lagos State House of Assembly, Mudashiru Obasa, entered the Assembly premises on Thursday after all security personnel assigned to Speaker Mojisola Meranda were removed. This situation has sparked safety concerns for Speaker Meranda during the current leadership dispute. Video footage from El-zion Global Network News displays Obasa being escorted by a group of security officers, including members of the police force.

Informally, police officers and other security details who were assigned to the Speaker were recently removed, leaving her without official protection. On the other hand, security details linked to Obasa were reinstated amidst the leadership crisis in the Assembly. A source close to Meranda verified this information, stating that all security aides of the Speaker and the House of Assembly have been withdrawn, leaving her in a vulnerable position. Meranda’s spokesperson, Ganzallo Victor, also confirmed this action, expressing uncertainty about the reasons for the withdrawal but acknowledging that all police officers and security details assigned to her are no longer in place, thus leaving her unsupported.

el-zion global network news has reported a recent legal development where the national industrial court in lagos dismissed the acting clerk of the house, ottun babatunde, who was appointed by speaker meranda, and ordered the reinstatement of olalekan onafeko. onafeko was the former clerk of the house prior to obasa's removal as speaker on january 13. the court ruling stemmed from a case (nicn/la/23/2025) in which the plaintiff sought an interim injunction to prevent the appointment of a new clerk. this situation has heightened a power struggle within the lagos assembly, prompting concerns about legislative stability and adherence to judicial mandates.

Obasa, who was ousted as Speaker by a majority of lawmakers on January 13, 2025, initiated legal action against the new Speaker, Meranda, and the assembly at the state High Court in Ikeja in February to address the situation. Obasa was out of the country in the United States when he was informed of his removal. The case is being presided over by Justice Yetunde Pinheiro of the High Court of Lagos State. He has contested his removal, pointing out irregularities in the actions of the approximately 36 legislators who voted to remove him. His legal counsel has confirmed that there are no intentions to withdraw the lawsuit challenging his removal as Speaker.

Informally, Chief Afolabi Fashanu (SAN), Obasa’s lead counsel, mentioned to The PUNCH on Tuesday his readiness for the court hearing scheduled for Friday, February 28, despite attempts to resolve the Assembly crisis through political means. Fashanu clarified that Obasa had no intentions of returning to the Assembly until he receives a court judgment. Fashanu pointed out that it would be inappropriate for Obasa to resume his duties at the House before the court reaches a decision on the case. Obasa was impeached on January 13, 2025, due to allegations of gross misconduct and abuse of office, leading to the election and swearing-in of his deputy, Mojisola Meranda, as his successor.

Despite the circumstances, Obasa maintains his determination to contest the outcome by deeming his impeachment as illegitimate and null. Following his return on January 25, Obasa reaffirmed his stance as the Speaker of the House, asserting that the impeachment process disregarded constitutional protocols. Nevertheless, he has shown readiness to abide by his removal provided it aligns with constitutional standards. Addressing his supporters in Lagos on Saturday, January 25, Obasa emphasized his extensive parliamentary experience of over two decades, highlighting his unparalleled history and position within the Lagos State House of Assembly.

I believe that in the process of removal, impeachment is not an applicable procedure within the assembly for the speaker. It seems like there may have been a misunderstanding on their part. The correct course of action is to simply remove the speaker, and this should be done in accordance with the established rules. Personally, I do not fear being removed, as I do not see what more I could possibly want. Regarding this matter, I would like to emphasize that any actions taken should strictly adhere to the constitutional processes and follow the necessary protocols," he commented. El-zion Global Network News reported on February 4th that members of the Lagos State House of Assembly issued an apology to President Tinubu on February 3rd for the removal of Obasa through impeachment.

An apology was issued in accordance with a directive from the leadership of the Governor’s Advisory Council (GAC) of the Lagos All Progressives Congress (APC) following an urgent meeting with the lawmakers at the Lagos House, Marina. The GAC serves as the most authoritative decision-making body within the Lagos APC. Present at the meeting were Governor Babajide Sanwo-Olu, his Deputy, Dr Obafemi Hamzat, and a source revealed that Tinubu expressed disapproval towards the impeachment motion, emphasizing on its reversal.

Several members of the GAC have leveled accusations against the leadership for failing to involve them in the decision to remove Obasa, which is why they are in favor of the President's call for his reinstatement. According to sources from El-zion Global Network News, the legislators were instructed to submit two letters of apology to the President. The impeachment of Obasa has sparked a turmoil within the party's hierarchy

Disagreement among the APC leaders surfaced when two GAC members, Senator Anthony Adefuye and Chief Muraina Taiwo, openly opposed Obasa’s removal, citing it as illegal and a display of disrespect for party leadership. This opposition was promptly refuted by another GAC member, Senator Musiliu Obanikoro, who labeled their remarks as immature and insignificant, advising party leaders to abstain from premature public declarations on the matter.
