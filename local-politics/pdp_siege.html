BODE GEORGE SLAMS POLICE SIEGE ON PDP SECRETARIAT, WARNS AGAINST TURNING NIGERIA INTO RUSSIA

EL-ZION NEWS   

Chief Bode George, former Deputy National Chairman (South) of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP), has expressed strong disapproval over the denial of access to members of the party’s Board of Trustees (BoT) at the PDP national secretariat in Abuja.

Security operatives had earlier taken over the PDP headquarters, preventing BoT members from entering the National Executive Committee (NEC) hall for their scheduled meeting.

As a result, the BoT was forced to relocate the meeting to the Yar’Adua Centre in Abuja’s Central Business District.

Reacting to the situation, George described the move as a deliberate attempt to undermine internal party democracy.

“They said we will not hold our meetings in our office? What is going on? We must avoid that pit-line, the madness. What is it? Can’t we sit and talk? What is the essence of democracy? Nigerians are watching,” he said.

He condemned the police presence at the party secretariat, calling it an infringement on the rights of party elders.
