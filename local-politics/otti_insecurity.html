OTTI VOWS TO TACKLE ESCALATING INSECURITY IN ABIA NORTH

EL-ZION NEWS   

During a monthly media interaction held Thursday in Umuahia, Governor Alex Otti expressed deep anger over the recent attack on the Commissioner for Lands and Housing, Mr. Chaka Chukwumerije, which occurred along the Umunneochi–Okigwe route in Imo State.

Chukwumerije, son of the late Senator Uche Chukwumerije of Umunneochi, reportedly escaped unharmed from an encounter with criminals operating around the Okigwe/Umunneochi axis, though the exact circumstances remain unclear.

Addressing the press, the Commissioner for Information, Prince Okey Kanu, confirmed that security agencies were already handling the matter, adding that sensitive security details could not be disclosed at the moment.

Security challenges, particularly intense in the Umunneochi corridor around October 2023, had been significantly reduced following the introduction of a special operation tagged "Operation Crush."

Governor Otti also acknowledged a recent security breach around Isuikwuato and vowed harsh consequences for those responsible, reiterating his administration’s firm stance against criminal activity.

“In the last few days, we have had reports of security breaches in Umunneochi and some parts of Isuikwuato, to the extent that one of our Commissioners was attacked on the stretch between Umunneochi and Umuahia, and the Imo State part of the stretch,” Otti stated. “So what we have done is to double our efforts and make sure that we secure Umunneochi. And those who come from there will agree that in the last few days, things have changed drastically.”

Emphasizing the significance of public safety, the Governor said, “It is our strong opinion that everything we do will succeed or fail on the altar of security, or lack thereof. So we take security very, very seriously.”

He also disclosed that security forces raided a criminal hideout in the Ugwunagbo area near Aba, leading to several arrests and the destruction of the camp. Unfortunately, a soldier lost his life during the operation.

“You may or may not have heard that within the month, a camp that existed in Ugwunagbo was attacked by security agencies. They recovered so much, and unfortunately, we lost a soldier. But the casualties on the part of the criminals were a whole lot, including the people we arrested. And we brought down the camp,” he revealed. “So we want to state that as government, we have zero tolerance for robbery, and all such crimes. And we continue to get ourselves prepared to deal with that.”

Turning attention to the health sector, Governor Otti noted that his administration had made significant progress in transforming primary health care delivery under Project Ekwueme.

“The Project Ekwueme, where we are renovating 200 primary health care centers, is on course. A lot of them are now ready for use. We’ve commissioned a few because there are a lot of them,” he said. “All our health centers are properly equipped to attend to people who go there. It’s only if the issues are complicated that they will have to be referred to the secondary and tertiary health centers. Deliveries happen in those primary health care centers.”

He added, “So the kind of equipment you expect to see in developed countries are already here in other states, and there is none that we are opening without equipping them properly. Once we finish equipping them, we’ll give them authorization to open.”

The Governor also reported ongoing upgrades to secondary and tertiary health facilities across the state.

“Most of them are receiving attention. The general hospital in Aba is already working. The Mother and Child Hospital at Ugwunagbo, the federal hospital handed over to the state to operate, is working very well,” he said. “Of course, some others are at different stages of renovation — the ones at Arochukwu, Okikpe, the ones in Obingwa, and a few others.”

Speaking about the Abia State University Teaching Hospital, he stated, “The phase two of the renovation has started. Some new facilities are being built so that they will be upgraded from where we took them by December 2023, when we receive the accreditation or restore the certification that was lost in 2021. Having done that, we thought we should still upgrade it so that it can function effectively as a tertiary health institution. And of course, the Medical City project will soon be flagged off. I’m happy to report that the access route to this site has been completed, and streetlights have been installed.”

On the education front, the Governor highlighted remarkable improvements, including the recruitment of over 5,000 new teachers.

“On education, the 5,394 teachers recruited in the first round are going through orientation in Aba and Umuahia. The orientation will end tomorrow (Friday), and thereafter, they will be deployed,” he said. “The next set of recruitments will happen immediately after this orientation, and we expect that the portal will be open and applications will be received.”

He revealed plans to hire an additional 4,000 teachers in preparation for the next academic session, noting the increased school enrollment following the implementation of free and compulsory education.

“We expect that about 4,000 additional teachers will be recruited in that process. The whole idea is to prepare ourselves for the next academic session, given the massive enrollment that we have witnessed since we introduced the free and compulsory education at the beginning of this year. We need to prepare and ensure that we have the right number and quality of teachers for our primary and secondary schools.”

He also mentioned ongoing renovation of several schools, including Abia State University, and announced that 20 schools would be upgraded into smart schools.

“The renovation of schools continues. In addition to the renovation, we are converting 20 schools into smart schools, and work is ongoing,” Otti said. “Also, at Abia State University, the renovation of the hostels is nearing completion, and we are also building additional hostels like we announced earlier, and we are getting ready for the faculties of agriculture and law to move to Uturu main campus.”

He concluded by confirming the restoration of power to the university and the development of an embedded power park.

“Light has been restored to the University of Uturu, while an embedded park is also being worked on. I think that’s going to be about 15 to 20 megawatts, and the idea is not just for Abia State University to have constant power, but also the environment in Uturu to benefit as a host of the university.”
