ADC LAUNCHES MOVE TO ASSUME CONTROL OF PDP STRUCTURES IN NORTHERN STATES

EL-ZION NEWS   

As part of its efforts to forge a strong political alliance ahead of the 2027 general elections, the African Democratic Congress (ADC) has begun reaching out to top politicians from both the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) and the All Progressives Congress (APC).

The ADC has already taken control of PDP structures in Yobe, Gombe, Adamawa, and several other northern states. However, many of the high-profile figures being courted are yet to make formal declarations of defection.

In response to recent reports, Governor Babagana Zulum of Borno State dismissed claims that he and five other APC governors were planning to defect to the ADC, calling the reports baseless.

Similarly, Labour Party’s 2023 presidential candidate, Mr. Peter Obi, reaffirmed his commitment to his party and supporters, stressing that his involvement in coalition-building does not indicate a change in party allegiance.

Meanwhile, tensions rose after the Minister of the Federal Capital Territory, Nyesom Wike, challenged ADC’s Interim National Secretary, Rauf Aregbesola, to account for his past performance as Interior Minister.

ADC Gains Ground in Northern States

Confirming the ADC’s integration of PDP structures in Yobe, Gombe, and Adamawa, Paul Ibe, media aide to former Vice President Atiku Abubakar, said executives in those states had aligned with the coalition.

Ibe stated: “The movement is gaining traction. Atiku Abubakar will continue to provide direction to ensure the coalition evolves into a formidable, robust, and viable platform. Having achieved that, you can already see the results. The Yobe PDP executive has aligned with the ADC, as has Gombe’s. The Adamawa executives have also pledged their loyalty. The movement is gaining momentum.”

He commended Atiku’s leadership role, adding: “Just last week, the political landscape was trending towards a one-party dictatorship, reminiscent of the Abacha era. This coalition is a response to that threat.”

He compared the process of building a coalition to the experience of newly married couples learning to adjust to each other: “That’s the nature of politics. Even a young marriage comes with its challenges. You’ve lived independently or been distant, and now you have to share space with someone from a completely different background. Perhaps the person you’re with isn’t even from your region, maybe they’re Igbo and you’re Igala. All these factors come into play, and you begin to learn to live together, tolerate one another, and make compromises. It’s akin to the struggles young couples face at the start of a marriage. But over time, things improve. He (Atiku) will continue to provide direction and ensure the coalition gains traction at all levels: ward, local government, and state. By the end of this year, anyone standing in the way of this coalition will have himself to blame.”

PDP Dismisses Defections

However, Austin Nwachukwu, Chairman of the PDP State Chairmen Forum and PDP Chairman in Imo State, downplayed reports of defections, calling them “good riddance” and asserting that the party remains strong and united.

Nwachukwu said: “The chairmen of Yobe and Borno vehemently denied these claims. These are fake news designed to attract public sympathy. Many of these politicians are over 70 years old. What are they still looking for? Retirement funds, not service to the nation.”

He insisted that the PDP remains resilient and dismissed the defections as insignificant. “Many of those who left have returned to the PDP. We wish those in the ADC well, but the PDP is stronger than ever,” he said.
