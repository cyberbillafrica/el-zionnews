I Can't stop anyone from dumping APC ,....Buhari

In a significant development within the All Progressives Congress (APC), it has been reported that former President Muhammadu Buhari has declined the appeal made by APC governors to prevent members from leaving the party. The issue stemmed from a recent meeting between Buhari and the APC governors, where concerns were raised regarding the departure of the Congress for Progressive Change (CPC) bloc from the party.

According to reports from El-zion News, the meeting took place at Buhari's Kaduna Residence on Monday. The decision of Buhari to uphold his stance on the matter has led to a sense of unease and uncertainty within the APC, as the potential exodus of the CPC bloc could have significant implications for the party's future.Furthermore, El-zion News brought to light statements made by Mallam Nasir El-rufai, who indicated that his decision to leave the APC was influenced by Buhari's support. This development underscores the complexities and dynamics at play within Nigeria's political landscape, with implications that could shape the future trajectory of the APC.

There is a prevailing belief within public discourse that the recent renovation of the Kaduna Residence of President Buhari by President Tinubu carries deep political implications. This action has sparked speculation that it may signify an attempt to appease President Buhari in light of perceived grievances, such as alleged anti-Northern policies, allegedly biased appointments, and the relocation of certain Federal Government agencies from Abuja to Lagos—an act that some Northern politicians view as a direct challenge to their interests.

The nuances of this renovation project have stirred conversations about the intricate dynamics that exist within Nigerian politics, particularly those involving regional interests and power play. The significance attributed to this gesture underscores the complexities of leadership relations and political strategies within the nation's landscape.

As the discourse continues, it remains essential to analyze these developments through a lens of political history, strategic alliances, and the broader socio-political context to fully grasp the potential implications and ramifications of such actions in the Nigerian political sphere.In the political landscape of Nigeria, tensions are running high within the All Progressives Congress (APC). The Congress for Progressive Change (CPC), led by the former President Buhari, wields considerable influence within the party. Recent rumblings within the party suggest that there is a growing rift between the North and the South, particularly in their search for a suitable presidential candidate.

Reports indicate that the Northern faction is seeking a Southern candidate who is willing to commit to serving a single term of four years as President, with the understanding that power will then transition back to the North in 2031. President Buhari's purported acceptance of this arrangement has only intensified the unease within the APC, particularly in the camp of Bola Tinubu, a prominent figure in the party.President Buhari's diplomatic stance on members leaving the APC has been viewed with skepticism, with many interpreting it as tacit approval of the proposed power-sharing agreement. As the political landscape continues to evolve, it remains to be seen how these internal tensions will impact the future trajectory of the APC and the broader political landscape in Nigeria.
