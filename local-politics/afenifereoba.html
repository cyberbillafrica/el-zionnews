Afenifere faction appoints Oba Olaitan as Adebanjo’s replacement

A faction of Afenifere, a pan-Yoruba sociopolitical organization, has named Oba Oladipo Olaitan as its new leader.

He succeeds Pa Ayo Adebanjo, who recently passed away at the age of 96.

The announcement was made in a communique released after the faction’s monthly general meeting, which took place at Adebanjo’s residence in Isanya Ogbo, Ijebu-Ode, Ogun State.

The communique was signed by Chief Sola Ebiseni, the Secretary-General, and Justice Faloye, the National Publicity Secretary.

During the meeting, members reviewed the National Caucus's recommendations and approved Oba Olaitan’s appointment as the new leader of the faction.

“Oba Olaitan will function as the Leader in acting capacity till after the final burial of Papa Ayo Adebanjo when he will assume the office as substantive Leader,” the statement reads.

In his acceptance speech, the new leader, who was deputy to Adebanjo, committed “himself to the ideals of Afenifere as a social-political organisation and unrelenting advocate of restructuring and true federalism, adding that he would ensure the unity of the organisation.”

A lawyer called to the Nigerian Bar in 1971, Oba Olaitan previously served as Political Adviser to Governor Lateef Jakande and was a member of the Lagos State Executive Council from 1979 to 1983.

He has been part of Afenifere for over 45 years, holding key positions such as National Financial Secretary under Chief Reuben Fasoranti and Deputy Leader under Chief Ayo Adebanjo.
