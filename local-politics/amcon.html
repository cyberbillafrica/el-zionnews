AMCON has recovered ₦2.1 trillion for the Federal Government and vow to recover more.

During a recent media briefing in Lagos, the Asset Management Corporation of Nigeria (AMCON) announced that it has successfully recovered more than N2.1 trillion for the Federal Government, despite encountering significant resistance from debtors. The recovery agency shared that it is currently handling over 2,000 cases in court, highlighting instances where the borrowed funds were not utilized for their intended purposes. Mr. Jude Nwauzor, the Head of Corporate Communications at AMCON, reiterated the corporation's dedication to retrieving outstanding debts, particularly emphasizing the N227.6 billion owed by Arik Air Limited (in Receivership) and its founder, Sir Johnson Arumemi-Ikhide.

"In light of the ongoing legal battles and allegations of mismanagement, AMCON has restated its dedication to recovering the N227.6 billion owed by Arik Air Limited (in Receivership) and its founder, Sir Johnson Arumemi-Ikhide. The agency recognizes the significance of the media in achieving our recovery objectives, as many debtors tend to present distorted narratives in an attempt to discredit AMCON. These debtors have had a detrimental impact on the Nigerian economy and it is crucial that the media does not give them a platform to manipulate public perception," remarked Nwauzor.

Mr. Nwauzor acknowledged that due to the media's role as a reflection of society, the leadership of AMCON is firmly convinced that enhancing the partnership between AMCON and the media will yield favorable outcomes for the nation. Additionally, he mentioned that the debt recovery process has been strenuous and demanding. He expressed disappointment over the obstacles faced by AMCON, citing that many Nigerian companies are hesitant to settle their debts without engaging in legal disputes.

AMCON is still encountering pushback from several debtors who are reluctant to settle their debts without resistance, he stated. He mentioned that one of these debtors is Arik Air Limited (currently in Receivership), an airline owned by Sir Johnson Arumemi-Ikhide, who also promotes Rockson Nigeria Limited, Ojemai Farms Limited, and Ojemai Investment Limited. The debts of these companies were transferred to AMCON by various banks due to their failure to meet obligations, amounting to a total of N455,171,764,772.80 as of December 31, 2024. Arik Air owes AMCON N227,637,469,394.34; Rockson Engineering owes N163,502,837,397.75, and Ojemai Farms owes N14,031,457,980.71.
