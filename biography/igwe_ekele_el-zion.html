Autobiography of El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe

El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe, just like many of his associates call him, is a man of creativity and many visions. He was once addressed as Joseph the Dreamer. He believes that everything is possible. His desire to replicate the excellence in media and broadcasting seen in well-developed climes was the drive that made him establish El-zion Global Network Tv.

El-zion, from his teenage years, has been a media enthusiast, and has exhibited passion for various aspects of media, such as journalism, filmmaking, content creation, and digital marketing. Being an author who engages himself in in-depth research helped him develop a knack for excellence in everything he delves into. He is naturally creative. He used his Android phone to design graphics and edit eye-catching videos during the early days of El-zion Global Network Tv.He doesn't hesitate to tell people who have had the privilege to attend his seminar that El-zion Global Network TV station is a product of faith aided by his android phone.

He doesn't allow circumstances to determine his next action. To the surprise and disbelief of people around him, he set out on the journey of establishing what is now one of the most digitized TV stations without having a four-wall office. Suffice it to say, El-zion Global Network TV started in a one-room self-contained apartment where he was living with his dear wife Pst Mrs Chika Florence Nnanna-Igwe, within the staff quarters of Eastern Nigeria Medical Center Uwani Enugu, Enugu State, Nigeria.El-zion Global Network TV is one among many dreams which El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe is determined to actualize.

Today El-zion Global Network TV boasts state-of-the-art media equipment and highly talented professionals in the field of broadcasting and media. This set of dedicated staff is being led by Mrs. Chika Nnanna-Igwe, who is the co-founder and the managing director of El-zion Global Network TV.

BIOGRAPHY

Born on 5th March 1980 to the family of Chief Nnanaa Igwe and Pst. Mrs Amaechi Comfort Igwe of Umuokwafor Umuchoke Amaigbo, Nwangele Local Government of Imo State, El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe started early in life to enjoy divine favor. The then Governor of Enugu State, Chief Ifeanyichukwu Nwodo, initiated a program tagged Free Child Delivery in government-owned hospitals in Enugu State, of which University of Nigeria Teaching Hospital UNTH in Enugu, where El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe was born, happened to be one of the designated hospitals. Giving birth was free of charge during that period. Fortunately, El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe was among the children born during that grace period, indicating that he was born a man of grace.

Being the eldest and having five male siblings, El-zion from his early teenage years identified with God. His parents' stance and beliefs in God made him deeply engaged in participating in church activities. This led to him being one of the ushers who participated during the 1992 Bread of Life Mission national convention that took place at Family Circle Center. At the age of 12, being among men and women as a dedicated usher directing people to their seats was a wake-up call to the youths and adults who were nonchalant towards working for God.

EDUCATION

El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe is one person who has a very clear vision of where he is going and what God wants him to become in life. That consciousness has made him remain unwavering in the face of odds and challenges towards the actualization of his educational dreams. One day, while he was still a primary two pupil at Agbani Road Primary School in Enugu, during the morning assembly, Ekele Igwe, as he was fondly called by his teacher and other pupils, was asked by his teacher, Mrs. Uwasomba, what he would like to become when he grows up. To the surprise of both teachers and other pupils, the courageous Ekele Igwe shouted, 'Doctor!' The teachers were surprised that the consciousness of becoming a medical doctor had become so strong in such a young boy that he didn't even wait for the teacher to finish the question before he thundered his response with unusual confidence that's rare among his peers.

At that tender age, El-zion Ekele Igwe was among the voices heard on an air live program. Because he continued exhibiting rare talent in his days at Agbani Road Primary School, he was among the six pupils who were selected to represent Agbani Road Primary School at Federal Radio Corporation of Nigeria (Radio Nigeria) in Enugu.

From Primary four till his graduation, El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe was among the group that represented Agbani Road Primary School during the annual Children's Day marching parade competition that takes place every 27th May at Michael Okpara's Independence Layout in Enugu. He had so much talented confidence that after 27th May he would be asked to join the second marching group that marches during the annual 1st October Nigeria's Independence Celebration March past, again at the same venue. Academically, he is among the top 8 students in every exam during his primary school days.

After his six years in primary school at Agbani Road Primary School in Enugu from 1986-1991, the young El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe proceeded to the then Maryland Boys Secondary School in Enugu. His talents and confidence didn't wane while he was at Maryland Boys Secondary School. His rare fair complexion always makes him stand out among his peers. His boldness and academic performance didn't allow him to spend much time before he was discovered by his then Form master, Mr. Oforkansi, who is now Bar Oforkansi. He is always among the few names that come to mind when there is a competition that has to do with intellectual capacity. He was among the top students that Mr. Oforkansi enlisted when he formed MAPET THEATRE. El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe was the Chief Speaker of a highly attended debate organized by Mr. Oforkansi. The theme of that debate was 'WE ARE HAPPIER THAN OUR FOREFATHERS.'As the Chief Speaker of the opposing side, young El-zion Ekele Igwe with his well-articulated points and rare comportment that are quite unique among his contemporaries made his side to win the debate by a large margin.

El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe had only junior secondary education at Maryland Boys Secondary School Enugu from 1992-1994. Considering his intelligence level, both of his parents decided that the young El-zion needed a more competitive high school that would be able to bring out the best in him. That decision led them through the rigorous and financially demanding process of getting El-zion admitted into the prestigious and highly academically celebrated College of Immaculate Conception (CIC) in Enugu.

During his Senior Secondary school at CIC Enugu, El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe kept on soaring higher. During his SS3 Class year, while his peers were trying to register for SSCE (WAEC) Examination, he went to register to his utmost dismay, and found out that his name was omitted from the list. He tried to enquire from his then Form mistress, who has the authority to authorize that very list, but she refused to come up with an explanation.

Young El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe, being full of confidence, decided to handle the situation without raising an alarm and decided to go for an alternative Exam known as WAEC GCE. Unfortunately, he didn't get all his subjects. In a bid not to be idle while waiting for another Exam Dates, El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe delved into the art of painting buildings. While he started traveling to different places to do this work and earning a living through it, he still has his eyes glued on becoming a Medical Doctor .Sometime in early 2002, someone approached El-zion and promised to help him towards being admitted to the College of Medicine at the University of Nigeria, Nsukka through what is called VC's list. After spending two years both at Nsukka and Enugu Campuses of the University, upon realizing that the admission was fake, even when people around were trying to persuade El-Zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe that it can be rectified, El-Zion decided to withdraw from the campus. Devastated but he didn't give up on his dream.

The painting and designing talents were so much on El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe that he was much sought after. Being a focused person, El-Zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe gradually started working his way back towards medical School To acquire deeper knowledge of God, between 2009 and 2011, El-zion enrolled in a Diploma in Theology at the prestigious Assemblies of God Divinity School of Nigeria (AGDSN) in Old Umuahia. Dr. Okpara Inworogu was the President of the School at that time l. On the first day he stepped into AGDSN, he was chosen to work with the Registrar of the College, Dr. John Whyte Ede. His academic intelligence led his then class representative, Reverend Uche Kalu, to start calling him Obama. This was prompted by Obama being the President of the United States of America at that time and considered intelligent. His insatiable quest for knowledge about God led him to enroll in the Redeemed Christian Church of God School of Disciples (SOD). After that, he enrolled in Living Faith's Word of Faith Bible Institute. In all these classes, he always emerged as one of the best.

The knowledge he acquired in these theological schools helped El-zion when he was tapped on the shoulder to become the Pastor in charge of New Covenant parish of Redeemed Christian Church of God, Akwuke Road Enugu. New Covenant parish was considered the fastest-growing parish among those planted at the same time. This was made possible by the grace of God upon El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe.

In a bid to achieve straight results, El-zion retook the SSCE WAEC in 2022 at Santa Maria Secondary School, Inyi, Oji River, Enugu State. After that, he was admitted for a Bachelor's Degree in Health Science at California Baptist University, California, USA. This will serve as the prerequisite degree, as he has a bigger dream this time: studying medicine at the prestigious Harvard Medical School. El-zion strongly believes that not even age can stop his dream. El-zion Ekeledilichukwu Igwe has never failed to trust in God and believe in himself.
