Ohanaeze criticizes FAAN for abrupt Enugu Airport closure over Easter.

The Ohanaeze Ndigbo Worldwide, the preeminent Igbo socio-cultural organization, has conveyed its dismay and dissatisfaction regarding the abrupt closure of the Akanu Ibiam International Airport (AIIA) in Enugu by the Federal Airports Authority of Nigeria (FAAN) over the Easter festive season. FAAN recently announced a necessary two-week emergency closure of the Enugu airport for essential runway rehabilitation due to a significant rupture in the asphalt surface, with the closure slated to occur from April 22nd to May 6th, 2025. The Director of Affairs and Consumer Protection stated in a press release that emergency repairs on the affected section of the runway are imperative, leading to the mandated closure in accordance with Nigerian civil aviation regulations.

Nevertheless, in a press release issued on Monday by the spokesperson of the organization, Dr. Ezechi Chukwu, Ohanaeze characterized the closure as being "unpatriotic and unfair" towards the regular airport users, particularly the Ndigbo community who tend to travel in large numbers during this period. Despite understanding that safety considerations may have been the cause, Ohanaeze pointed out that the closure's timing could have been better coordinated.

This is the third instance of the runway malfunctioning in less than 15 years. We urge President Bola Tinubu to step in and allocate funds for the comprehensive reconstruction of the entire runway, as opposed to the temporary fixes that have proven ineffective over time," stated Ohanaeze.

The organization lauded the Enugu State Government for its supportive actions and urged the administration under President Bola Tinubu to finalize the airport's rehabilitation and operationalize it in accordance with global standards.Ohanaeze Ndigbo Worldwide has expressed its concern regarding the Federal Airports Authority of Nigeria's (FAAN) management of the runway closure at the Akanu Ibiam International Airport in Enugu. The closure coinciding with the busy Easter festive season has been deemed unpatriotic and unfair by the leadership of Ohanaeze Ndigbo Worldwide. While safety concerns are acknowledged, the timing of the closure could have been handled more effectively.

Concern is expressed over the recurrent failures of the runway at the Akanu Ibiam International Airport, and a request is hereby made to President Bola Tinubu for the allocation of funds to reconstruct the entire runway. The plea is for a comprehensive reconstruction rather than temporary fixes that have historically proven ineffective. Furthermore, attention is brought to the incomplete state of the airport and the inadequate progress made by previous federal administrations. The Enugu State Government's efforts are commended, and there is a call upon the President Bola Tinubu Administration to ensure the full completion and operation of the airport in accordance with global standards.
