FBI OPENS INVESTIGATIONS INTO THREE CHILDREN'S HOSPITALS OVER ALLEGED GENITAL MUTILATION OF MINORS

EL-ZION NEWS   

The FBI has launched criminal investigations into three children’s hospitals following Attorney General Pam Bondi’s commitment to enforce federal laws prohibiting female genital mutilation, aiming to protect minors from what are described as often irreversible sex-change procedures.

According to a source familiar with the investigation, the hospitals under scrutiny include Boston Children’s Hospital, Children’s Hospital Colorado, and Children’s Hospital Los Angeles. These institutions have reportedly been among the leading providers of gender transition treatments for minors in recent years.

Shortly after assuming office, President Donald Trump signed an executive order directing all federal agencies to work toward ending the availability of “irreversible medical interventions” for children under 18 diagnosed with gender dysphoria. In response, Attorney General Bondi issued a memorandum directing Justice Department staff to enforce 18 U.S.C. § 116, a federal statute that criminalizes female genital mutilation.

“I am putting medical practitioners, hospitals and clinics on notice: In the United States, it is a felony to perform, attempt to perform or conspire to perform female genital mutilation (‘FGM’) on any person under the age of 18,” Bondi wrote. “That crime carries a maximum prison sentence of 10 years per count. I am directing all U.S. Attorneys to investigate all suspected cases of FGM — under the banner of so-called ‘gender-affirming care’ or otherwise — and to prosecute all FGM offenses to the fullest extent possible.”

The memo also announced the formation of a new Coalition Against Child Mutilation, which will collaborate with state attorneys general to build legal cases against individuals and institutions found violating laws prohibiting FGM. The Justice Department is also working on legislation to create a private right of action for children and parents whose “healthy body parts have been damaged by medical professionals through chemical and surgical mutilation,” allowing them to pursue retroactive legal action against providers.
