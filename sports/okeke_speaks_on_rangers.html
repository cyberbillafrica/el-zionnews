Okeke Reveals What Governor Mbah Hasn't Been Told About Enugu Rangers

EL-ZION NEWS   

The 2024/25 Nigeria Premier Football League season concluded two weekends ago, with Remo Stars clinching their first-ever title. Meanwhile, Enugu Rangers, the defending champions, saw their title challenge crumble well before the season's end. Despite starting strong, their campaign faltered, ending with the team failing to secure a continental spot. This decline is even more puzzling given that coach Fidelis Ilechukwu—who led them to previous glory—remained in charge.

Emmanuel Okeke, a devoted Enugu Rangers supporter popularly known as Okwuluora ndi Igbo, believes the club’s collapse suggests deeper problems. He highlighted their loss to Heartland FC as a red flag, calling the goals in that match “magical.”

Okeke said, “Four days before that match, I got phone calls from Heartland fans that they were going to beat Rangers 2-0; they said there was an arrangement. I questioned, which arrangement? When they hosted the match they won but the manner they got their goals was magical.”

He continued: “Some of you don’t know how much I love Rangers. I forfeited the chance of going to America for the first time and chose to go watch Rangers. After that defeat, I withdrew my support for Rangers to some extent.” He urged the club’s leadership to accept responsibility. “We need someone to come and tell us what happened in that match. Rangers is our club; Rangers is our conscience, Rangers is our joy; Rangers is everything. People need to take responsibility.”

Reflecting on their match against Enyimba, Okeke said the team gave a strong performance and showed real intent to win, unlike the Heartland game, which he described as shocking. He urged the people of Enugu to demand transparency because, in his words, “the club belongs to all of us.”

He also recalled telling journalists that Rangers would lose games in Benin, Rivers, and Ibadan—predictions that came true. “When I spoke with some of the players, they told me they were distraught and not motivated. Some of them cannot pay their rent. Truth must be told, we have not done well in terms of players’ welfare.”

Still, he didn’t spare the players either. “I expected them to do better. I don’t think they were helping themselves. If because a team is not paying you well and you refuse to do well, that is doing a great disservice to yourself, because if you do well you are selling yourself. If I am poorly paid and I am not doing well, how can I go to Real Madrid? They will never come for me. If you are not ready to play and die for Rangers, then leave,” he emphasised.

Drawing inspiration from Nwankwo Kanu’s early struggles at Ajax, Okeke said, “On getting to Amsterdam, Kanu said the weather was so unfriendly because it was too cold. Kanu remembered how he was hawking akara (bean cake) in Owerri. He then made a choice to remain under that cold and prove himself. Within months, Kanu, together with George Finidi, shot themselves to Ajax’s first team and subsequently won the Champions League. There are a lot of issues in Rangers that need to be addressed urgently.”

Asked whether Rangers could return to the top of Nigerian football, Okeke recalled, “In 2016, we won the league and in 2017 we were almost relegated. We had a match against Zanaco of Zambia; they couldn’t depart from Nigeria until a few hours before the match because money wasn’t released. In Zambia, they went straight from the airport to the pitch. They lost that match 4-0. One thing with us is getting to the top; another thing is how to sustain that height. Rangers need total overhaul. When Rangers lost that match people were crying and I noticed one player, Evans Ogbona, who covered his face in shame. He earned my respect. Rangers’ problem is lack of sustainability. Every day, you pay to go sit under the sun only to see your team lose. Bringing Rangers back will be very difficult."

When asked what advice he would give to the club’s leadership, Okeke responded, “They know the problem. The first thing I will say is to tell the General Manager to embark on a media tour and explain to the people what really happened during the season and explain the challenges as well. He shouldn’t wait for people to start pointing accusing fingers at him when he is not the problem. He should stop taking bullets for another person. Personally, I am ready to leave any position that will dent my image. We have seen Prime Ministers resign. I have seen a lot of coaches walk away because of poor results. Even if you are being paid $1 million you should leave when things are not working because people’s emotions are at stake. Each time Rangers lose I feel sad."

He criticized the General Manager’s dual role as an administrator and player: “Again, why is the General Manager playing? He has a very big task on his hands and I see that (playing) as a distraction. The Rangers’ project is too big to be shared with other duties. Wearing that jersey alone is a distraction. You are the GM, you sit at the VIP, you don’t sit around the coach. Club chairmen, top officials of clubs, sit in the VIP lounge, high up there. You don’t see them around the pitch. The guy is doing too much and it is beginning to tell on him. He should suspend playing and focus on this project."

Okeke called for internal reflection, urging the GM to “go back to the drawing board” and ask hard questions: “Why are the players complaining? Why are they looking poor? Why are they not feeding well? All the Enugu Rangers buses are in bad shape. In terms of players’ welfare, Rangers have not tried at all. Although this situation is common in the Nigerian league. At times, I sit and wonder, how players in the Nigerian Premier Football League survive.”

On reconnecting with fans, he advised the club to hold a post-season address to explain what went wrong. “Even if they had finished 20th, the fans need to be told why the club performed the way it did. That is packaging, how a football club should be run. Rangers is a brand and do not deserve less.”

He also emphasized the need to improve the matchday experience and upgrade facilities at the Nnamdi Azikiwe Stadium. Okeke lamented the loss in the President’s Federation Cup, which crushed hopes after the league disappointment. “Imagine how the final would have been: Abakaliki FC against Enugu Rangers. That would have been an epic. An all-south-east final would have rekindled something else in the entire region.”

On logistics and professionalism, he decried substandard travel and accommodations for the team: “Back to the loss to Kwara United, a team that travelled for such a match should have been lodged in a hotel where the players could have balanced diets and rested before the encounter. They would have gone with their dietitian.”

He added, “Imagine in 1994, Iwuanyanwu Nationale were going to the CAF Champions League on a chartered plane. If by then a Nigerian club could go to the continent on a chartered flight, why should clubs in Nigeria be travelling for league matches on coaster buses? In 2025, Nigerian clubs should have been owning their own planes.”

Criticizing the state of Nigerian football, Okeke said, “It is said when the righteous are in authority the people rejoice. The present people that run our football lack the passion. I told the General Manager that I want to call some people so that we can raise one billion naira for Rangers but after the Heartland match, I changed my mind. I promoted the Heartland match with close to ten million naira. I printed posters, jerseys and the like. I am a human being.”

Reflecting on his own commitment to the club’s growth, he shared, “When I joined the Rangers crusade last season, I saw that Rangers jersey was the most sought after product in Nigerian football. Even abroad, people are ready to pay in dollars to get it but they don’t have enough. I wrote to club management that I wanted the franchise, when I sell I will remit the money to the club. I planned to build a shop like that of Manchester United along Ogwi Road, where you can have everything about the club—jerseys, banners, bangles and memorabilia. Nothing came out of it.”

He lamented internal resistance: “Some people feel I am becoming too popular with the Rangers project. Some feel I am beginning to do too much. My plan was to change the way things are being done at present. I want to digitise the marketing methods for Rangers products. I wanted to contact DHL, EMS so that if you are abroad and you order for a Rangers jersey, it can be delivered to you through courier service.”

Okeke recounted Rangers’ visit to Umuahia and how poor accommodations affected players. “Why were they packed three in one room? When I asked, I was told because the hotel was close to the stadium.”

He praised Remo Stars’ management and urged Rangers to aim higher: “Why would Enugu Rangers not do a trophy tour. We all saw what Honourable Kunle Soname did with Remo Stars. The trophy tour was Real Madrid or Manchester United standard. That is how a club is run.”

Finally, he appealed to the state government: “The governor of Enugu State is a businessman. He is not a football person. Maybe somebody has not told him that football is serious business. Maybe they are afraid of him. They should tell him if we qualify for Champions League, it will fetch us $500,000, Group stage $700,000, quarter final $2 million and so on.”

“We, the fans of Enugu Rangers, are appealing to the governor to take another look at Rangers, that some of us our lives depend on this team. All our efforts should not be in vain due to the poor management of the club. Rangers players are hungry, that is why they are not doing well.”
