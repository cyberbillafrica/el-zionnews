10 SUPPLEMENTS THAT MAY RELIEVE MENOPAUSE SYMPTOMS

EL-ZION NEWS   

SUPPLEMENTS MAY PROVIDE RELIEF FOR MENOPAUSE SYMPTOMS, BUT EVIDENCE IS LIMITED

Hot flashes, brain fog, night sweats, and difficulty sleeping are common symptoms during menopause. While hormone therapy is often used for relief, it may not be suitable for everyone. Some supplements may help ease these symptoms, though scientific support is still limited.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Omega-3s are essential polyunsaturated fats that the body cannot produce on its own. They must be consumed through diet or supplements. These include alpha-linolenic acid (ALA) from plant oils and nuts, and docosahexaenoic acid (DHA) and eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA) from fish and fish oil.

According to one systematic review, omega-3s may support memory, cognition, and brain circulation. However, results from another systematic review had mixed results on whether omega-3 can help with menopause symptoms of hot flashes and depressive symptoms. More research is needed.

Omega-3s can thin the blood and should be avoided by individuals taking anticoagulants or those allergic to fish or shellfish. Common side effects include bad breath, diarrhea, nausea, headache, heartburn, foul-smelling sweat, and an unpleasant taste.

Black Cohosh

Black cohosh is a North American plant traditionally used to ease hot flashes, vaginal dryness, and sleep problems. It is made from its roots and underground stems.

A review from 2022 found that black cohosh performed better than a placebo at treating hot flashes and night sweats. However, other studies are inconsistent, and experts advise caution when using supplements with limited scientific backing.

Side effects are usually mild but may include stomach upset, headache, rash, or low blood pressure. It should be avoided by people with liver problems or hormone-sensitive conditions like breast cancer.

Red Clover

Red clover contains isoflavones, which act like estrogen in the body. It is also called cow clover or meadow clover. A meta-analysis found that people taking red clover had about 1.73 fewer hot flashes per day compared to placebo.

Possible side effects include headache, nausea, rash, and muscle pain. Red clover should be avoided by individuals with hormone-sensitive conditions, such as breast cancer, endometriosis, uterine fibroids, ovarian or endometrial cancer.

Probiotics

Probiotics are generally safe and well-tolerated. Initial side effects like gas and bloating may resolve over time. However, people with weakened immune systems or serious health conditions should avoid probiotics, as they may face a higher risk of severe infections.

Dong Quai

Dong quai, also called female ginseng, is derived from the Angelica sinensis root and has been used in Traditional Chinese Medicine for over a thousand years to address reproductive and menopausal issues.

Research on dong quai’s effectiveness is limited. While older studies show that it may reduce hot flashes and night sweats when combined with other herbs, further evidence is needed to determine its impact when used alone.

Dong quai may cause increased sensitivity to sunlight, and people taking anticoagulants or with hormone-sensitive health conditions should avoid it.
